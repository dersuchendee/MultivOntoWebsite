Introduction back to ToC

The concept of multivocality has gained significant attention in the field of ontology development, as it recognizes the inherent diversity and multiplicity of perspectives within a given domain. This ontology aims to capture the various interpretations, viewpoints, and linguistic nuances associated with different stakeholders and communities within the Cultural Heritage domain. By representing multiple voices and perspectives explicitly, the multivocality ontology provides a comprehensive framework for understanding and reconciling diverse interpretations of domain concepts. Our work contributes to advancing the field of ontology engineering by providing a robust and flexible approach to model and manage multivocality, fostering a more inclusive and comprehensive representation of knowledge.
